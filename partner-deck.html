Mandate

Risk-adjusted compounding inside a client-owned structure.

Gain operates systematic multi-asset trading programs inside regulated brokerage accounts owned by each client. The objective is simple: compound capital in a fully liquid format without taking custody or pooling funds.

Portfolio Context

Portfolios are strong on exposure, weak on liquid diversification.

“Most investors treat volatility as a risk to be endured rather than an asset class to be harvested.”
Market observation

Source: Long Angle high-net-worth allocation research.

Where portfolios stay concentrated

  • Public equities remain the main liquid growth engine.
  • Private enterprise and real estate absorb a large share of conviction capital.
  • Fixed income provides ballast, but not much upside in volatile conditions.
  • Liquid strategies designed to benefit from volatility are often underrepresented.

Gain acts as a complementary sleeve: not a replacement for core holdings, but a liquid strategy that broadens how the portfolio earns.

Custody Structure

Clients keep ownership. Gain receives authority to trade, not to hold funds.

Structural advantages

  • Capital remains in segregated brokerage accounts.
  • Gain operates under Limited Power of Attorney.
  • No custody means less friction and less perceived opacity.
  • No lockups preserves client flexibility.
  • Clients can withdraw without needing to untangle pooled capital.

Takeaway

Clear separation of capital ownership and trading authority.

The bottom line The client story stays simple: “Your money stays in your account. Gain manages the trading mandate.”
